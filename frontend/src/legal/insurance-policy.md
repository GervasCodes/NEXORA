> **Template notice:** This document describes how insurance-style protection currently
> works on NEXORA using in-app mechanisms (the wallet ledger and dispute system) rather
> than a third-party underwritten insurance product. If NEXORA later purchases a
> commercial marketplace/cargo insurance policy, this document should be replaced with
> the actual policy's terms, reviewed by qualified counsel and your insurance broker.

## 1. What this policy is

NEXORA does not currently underwrite a separate, purchasable insurance product for
Buyers, Sellers, or Delivery Agents. Instead, financial protection against damaged
items, delayed deliveries, defective products, and failed deliveries is built into the
Platform's **dispute-resolution and wallet-reversal mechanism**, described here for
transparency. Sellers and Delivery Agents who want additional protection beyond what's
described below are encouraged to hold their own commercial insurance (e.g. goods-in-
transit, public liability, or courier insurance), which this policy does not replace.

## 2. What is protected, by whom

| Loss | Protection mechanism | Funded by |
|---|---|---|
| Buyer receives a damaged item | Refund or replacement via dispute resolution | Seller's wallet (or the Seller's own arrangement with their Delivery Agent, if the Agent was at fault — see the Delivery Liability Policy §6) |
| Buyer receives a defective / not-as-described item | Refund or replacement via dispute resolution | Seller's wallet |
| Order is significantly delayed | Discretionary compensation, or full refund if undelivered and the Buyer no longer wants it | Seller's wallet, or NEXORA goodwill credit at admin discretion for force-majeure delays |
| Order goes missing in transit | Refund or replacement via dispute resolution | Seller's wallet, recoverable by the Seller from their Delivery Agent per their own arrangement |
| Seller's wallet balance is insufficient to fund a refund | Refund still issued to the Buyer; the shortfall becomes a payable balance owed by the Seller, deducted from future wallet earnings before further withdrawals | Seller (deferred) |

## 3. What this does *not* cover

- **Buyer's own mishandling** after confirmed delivery.
- **Consequential or indirect losses** (e.g. a Buyer's lost time, a Seller's lost
  future sales, or a Delivery Agent's vehicle costs) beyond the value of the order
  itself, unless required by applicable consumer-protection law.
- **High-value or specialty goods** requiring their own dedicated insurance (e.g.
  jewellery, electronics above a threshold, perishables) — Sellers listing such goods
  should arrange their own coverage, since the standard wallet-reversal mechanism above
  may not fully cover a large single-item loss.
- **Force-majeure events** — see Delivery Liability Policy §4 — beyond discretionary
  goodwill compensation.
- **Fraudulent claims** — a dispute found to be filed in bad faith (e.g. staged damage,
  false non-delivery claims) is rejected and may result in account action against the
  claimant, per the Terms of Service §6.

## 4. How to make a claim

There's no separate insurance claim form — the dispute process **is** the claim
process:

1. Open a dispute from the relevant order (`damaged`, `delayed`, `defective`,
   `wrong_item`, `missing`, or `other`).
2. Attach photo evidence and a description.
3. Respond to any follow-up messages from the Seller or admin.
4. An admin resolves the case as a full refund, partial refund, replacement,
   compensation, or rejection with reason — this **is** the payout decision.

## 5. Delivery Agents

Delivery Agents engaged directly by a Seller (rather than by NEXORA) are not NEXORA
employees and are not covered by any NEXORA-held insurance policy for injury, vehicle
damage, or third-party liability while making a delivery. Sellers who run their own
Delivery Team are responsible for whatever arrangement — employment, contractor
agreement, or their own insurance requirement — they have with their Delivery Agents.
NEXORA strongly recommends Sellers and Delivery Agents carry appropriate personal
accident and third-party liability cover for delivery work.

## 6. Payment-processor-level protection

Some payment methods offered at checkout (e.g. card payments processed through Stripe,
or PayPal) carry their own buyer-protection programs independent of NEXORA. Where
available, a Buyer may also have recourse directly through their payment provider;
using that recourse instead of, or in addition to, NEXORA's dispute process is the
Buyer's choice, though NEXORA will always ask that an in-app dispute be tried first so
we can resolve it quickly.

## 7. Future changes

If NEXORA introduces a formal underwritten insurance product (for high-value orders,
Delivery Agent accident cover, or similar), it will be described in a revised version
of this document with its own terms, premium (if any), and claims process, separate
from the mechanism described above.

## 8. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the Platform evolves. Material changes will be flagged in
the Platform with a new effective date above.
