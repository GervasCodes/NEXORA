## 1. Scope

This Vendor Agreement ("**Agreement**") governs your participation as a Seller on the
NEXORA marketplace, in addition to the general Terms of Service, which also apply to
you. Where the two conflict on a Seller-specific matter, this Agreement controls.

## 2. Your status

You act as an **independent seller**, not an employee, agent, or partner of NEXORA.
You're responsible for your own business registration, licensing, and tax obligations
applicable to what you sell and where you sell it.

## 3. Listings

- You must list products you're legally entitled to sell, with accurate descriptions,
  pricing, images, and quantities. Misleading listings (wrong specs, bait-and-switch
  pricing, stock-photo substitution for real product photos where authenticity matters)
  are a breach of this Agreement.
- You're responsible for keeping stock counts accurate; repeatedly accepting orders you
  can't fulfil may result in listing or account restrictions.
- Restricted, counterfeit, stolen, or illegal goods may not be listed. NEXORA may remove
  a listing or suspend a store without prior notice where it reasonably believes this
  clause is being violated.

## 4. Orders, commission, and payouts

- When a Buyer's payment for your order is confirmed, the order proceeds — net of
  NEXORA's commission for that transaction — are credited to your in-app wallet.
- The commission rate is the rate configured in the Platform at the time of the order
  and may be shown to you in your Seller dashboard; NEXORA may change the commission
  rate for future orders with notice.
- You may request a withdrawal of your wallet balance per the withdrawal process in
  your Seller Wallet dashboard; withdrawals are subject to review and standard
  processing times.
- If an order is refunded (fully or partially) per Section 6, the corresponding amount
  is reversed from your wallet balance, including where a payout for that order has
  already been withdrawn — in which case the reversal is deducted from your future
  wallet balance.

## 5. Fulfilment and your Delivery Team

- You're responsible for packaging every order safely and adequately for the transit
  it will undergo, and for handing it to your Delivery Agent (whether a member of your
  own Delivery Team or another courier you engage) in the condition described in your
  listing.
- If you manage your own Delivery Team through the Platform, you're responsible for
  that team's conduct in the same way you're responsible for your own — see the
  **[Delivery Liability Policy](/legal/delivery-liability-policy)** for exactly how
  damage and delay responsibility is attributed between you and a Delivery Agent.
- You must hand orders to your courier within the timeframe implied by the delivery
  estimate shown to the Buyer at checkout. Repeated late handoffs are a breach of this
  Agreement.

## 6. Damaged items, defective products, and refunds — your obligations

You are the party responsible for:

- **Defective or not-as-described products**: if a Buyer receives an item that is
  defective, materially different from its listing, or otherwise not fit for its
  ordinary purpose, you must accept the dispute in good faith and provide one of a
  full refund, partial refund, or replacement, as resolved by the admin reviewing the
  case.
- **Items damaged before handoff to the courier**: if evidence shows the item was
  already damaged, or was packaged in a way that made transit damage foreseeable and
  avoidable, responsibility sits with you, not the Delivery Agent — see the
  attribution rules in the Delivery Liability Policy.
- **Refund funding**: refunds for the situations above are funded from your wallet
  balance per Section 4. This is why accurate listings and careful packaging directly
  protect your earnings.

You are **not** automatically responsible for damage that a courier caused in transit
after a proper handoff, or for delays caused solely by the Delivery Agent's route —
see the Delivery Liability Policy for the full attribution framework, and Section 7
of the Insurance Policy for how these losses may be covered.

## 7. Disputes involving your orders

When a Buyer opens a dispute against your order, you'll be notified and can respond
with messages and evidence through the dispute thread. Failing to respond to an open
dispute within a reasonable time may result in the admin resolving it without your
input, which can include a refund funded from your wallet.

## 8. Reviews and store standing

Buyers may rate and review completed orders. NEXORA may use dispute history, refund
rate, and review scores to determine store visibility, verification badges, or, in
cases of a sustained poor record, suspension of your seller account.

## 9. Suspension and termination

NEXORA may suspend or terminate your seller account for breach of this Agreement, the
Terms of Service, fraud, or a sustained pattern of unresolved disputes. On termination,
open orders must still be fulfilled or refunded, and your remaining wallet balance
(net of any pending refund liability) will be released to you per the standard
withdrawal process.

## 10. Changes to this Agreement

We may update this Agreement as the Platform evolves. Material changes will be flagged
in the Platform with a new effective date above; continuing to sell after a change
takes effect is acceptance of the updated Agreement.

## 11. Contact

Questions about this Agreement can be sent through the support contact listed in your
Seller dashboard.
